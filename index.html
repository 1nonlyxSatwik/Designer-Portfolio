Why Am I Here?
Honestly, I'm just a modal. I pop up, I ask for attention, and then usually I go away. What's your purpose today?
↑

It started with curiosity. I’ve always been the kind of person who asks “why” a little too much and thinks about things a little too deeply — basically an overthinker by default. Whether it’s figuring out how a website works, obsessing over tiny design details, or reimagining how something could be better, I naturally get pulled into ideas and keep digging. That curiosity slowly turned into building — experimenting with code, creating interfaces, and trying to make digital experiences feel alive rather than static. I’m especially drawn to things with motion, interaction, and personality — stuff that feels less like software and more like an experience.

A lot of my ideas show up late at night. Insomnia has weirdly become part of my creative process — some people sleep, I redesign things in my head at 2AM. I overthink layouts, interactions, random life decisions… pretty much everything. But honestly, that overthinking is also where a lot of creativity comes from. Outside coding, I’m a complete movie holic — I love stories, visuals, soundtracks, scenes that stay in your head. Films inspire how I think about emotion and atmosphere even in digital work. And games have always been a huge part of me too — not just playing them, but loving the worlds, mechanics, storytelling, and immersion they create.

Right now I’m a second-year CSE student exploring the space between development, design, and experimentation. I like building things that feel playful, immersive and a little unexpected. Still learning, still breaking things, still figuring stuff out — usually while juggling too many ideas, too many tabs open, and too little sleep. Overthinker, insomniac, movie addict, gamer… and always building something.

I am Satwik Mani Tripathi, a Computer Science student who found his true calling at the intersection of lines of code and pixels. For me, software has never been just about logic gates and algorithms; it is about human connection. I believe that design is not just what it looks like, but how it works and feels. My interest lies in crafting interfaces that feel alive, bridging the gap between complex frontend development and thoughtful UI/UX design. Every line of code I write is an attempt to translate an abstract concept into a functional product that someone, somewhere, can interact with and enjoy. I am deeply curious about how humans behave, why they click where they do, and how design systems can guide their journey. To me, a design system is not just a library of buttons; it is a shared language of constraints, intent, and storytelling. We live in an era where AI is redefining the canvas of software creation. I view AI not as a replacement for the human hand, but as an amplifier for creativity. I designed Fluxfolio to mirror this philosophy—to be a space where design and logic don't compromise with each other, but elevate one another.
