Why Am I Here?
Honestly, I'm just a modal. I pop up, I ask for attention, and then usually I go away. What's your purpose today?
↑

It started with curiosity.

As a kid, I was obsessed with how things were built — not just how they looked, but why they worked the way they did. I'd take apart objects, sketch ideas, and spend hours noticing small details most people overlooked. I didn't know it back then, but I was already thinking like a designer.

Before screens became my primary medium, I was deeply into products, machines, and form. I was fascinated by how a physical object could communicate intent through shape, material, and ergonomics. That interest slowly evolved from engines and hardware into interfaces and systems — from how things are made to how people experience them.

Studying Industrial Design helped me ground that curiosity in reality: research, ergonomics, prototyping, and manufacturing logic. Over time, UI/UX entered naturally not as a switch, but as an extension. I realised modern products don't live in one world anymore. A product is the hardware and the interface and the flow and the emotion.

Today, I design end-to-end product experiences — physical and digital — using a mix of industrial design thinking, UI/UX principles, and AI-assisted workflows. I enjoy working from first principles, asking uncomfortable questions, and turning complexity into clarity.
